# AI Art Newsletter

Here’s a short newsletter on recent AI art and exhibition news, with a mix of major museum shows, artist activity, and current debate around AI’s role in creativity. [abc.net](https://www.abc.net.au/news/2026-03-03/ai-art-caricature-impact-on-creative-workers/106382724)

## Headlines

- **Botto at Art Basel Hong Kong:** the autonomous AI artist Botto is showing live at Art Basel Hong Kong 2026 in the fair’s digital art section, drawing attention as one of the most visible AI art presentations this week. [gettyimages](https://www.gettyimages.dk/detail/video/guests-look-at-artwork-by-botto-at-art-basel-hong-kong-news-footage/2268635795)
- **Major museum exhibition in Sweden:** _AI and the Paradox of Agency_ opened at Bildmuseet in Umeå on 13 March 2026 and runs into 2027, featuring international artists exploring power, agency, and machine collaboration. [bildmuseet.umu](https://www.bildmuseet.umu.se/en/exhibitions/2026/ai-and-the-paradox-of-agency/)
- **Cannes exhibition on humans and machines:** _PERCEPTION LOOPS — Of Humans, Machines and Hybrid Nature_ opened in Cannes during EthCC, pairing AI, robotics, and physical artworks by aurèce vettier and Patrick Tresset. [upmag](https://upmag.com/press-release-ai-and-robotics-art-exhibition-perception-loops-opens-in-cannes-during-ethcc/)
- **UK community exhibitions:** recent UK shows include _Drawing the Line: An RAI Exhibition_ in Nottingham and an AI-driven exhibition at Mansfield Museum shaped by neurodivergent young people. [mansfield.gov](https://www.mansfield.gov.uk/news/article/13042/mansfield-museum-launches-new-ai-driven-exhibition-created-by-neurodivergent-young-people)

## What’s changing

AI art is increasingly moving from “novelty” into mainstream exhibition spaces, with museums, fairs, and cultural institutions treating it as a serious curatorial topic rather than a side attraction. [gla.ac](https://www.gla.ac.uk/news/headline_1254193_en.html)

At the same time, the conversation is getting sharper: some artists and commentators are pushing back on AI-generated work, while others argue for hybrid practice, consent-aware training, and stronger human authorship. [mixflow](https://mixflow.ai/blog/ai-by-the-numbers-march-2026-statistics-every-creative-needs-to-know-about-new-artistic-styles/)

## Why it matters

The latest shows suggest AI art is becoming more visible, more institutional, and more contested all at once. [edition.cnn](https://edition.cnn.com/2026/03/26/style/botto-ai-artist-art-basel-hong-kong-intl-hnk)
